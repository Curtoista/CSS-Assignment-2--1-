NHL Awards

The NHL has a long and storied history of all-time greats, with legends like "The Great One" Wayne Gretzky to the original offensive defenseman Bobby Orr piling up stats and trophies on a yearly basis. With each passing year, new faces come into the league vying for the ultimate goal in hockey, to hoist the Stanley Cup! But not everyone can achieve such a goal, and to celebrate all the special players the league has to offer the NHL hosts an awards ceremony at the culmination of each NHL season

Here are your 2022-2023 NHL Award winners:

2022-2023 NHL Season Recap

The 2022-2023 NHL Season has concluded and the Vegas Golden Knights are your Stanley Cup champions!

It was an exciting year all around as my hometown Boston Bruins managed to shatter records, finishing as the number one seed and breaking the all-time NHL points record for a single season. Ultimately their season ended in disappointment as they were upset in the first round of the Stanley Cup Playoffs by the eighth seeded Florida Panthers. The Panthers ended up going on a miraculous run all the way to the Stanley Cup Finals, ultimately ending in a 4-1 series loss to the Golden Knights.

Stanley Cup Playoffs

It was an exciting playoffs with upsets abound that ended with the expansion Vegas Golden Knights winning their first Stanley Cup in only their 6th season in the league. Top seeded teams in the Central and Atlantic divisons, the Colorado Avalanche and Boston Bruins respectively, were both upset in the first round by four seeds the Seattle Kraken and the Florida Panthers. The Panthers proceeded to go on a miraculous run all the way to the Stanley Cup Finals, ultimately losing out to the Knights 4-1.

NHL Draft

Every year a collection of new and upcoming talent is blessed with the opportunity of being drafted into the league via the NHL Draft. While the incoming class is decidedly a talented group, this year was known as the "Connor Bedard draft" as the 18 year-old from North Vancouver, Canada is widely recognized as a generational talent the likes of Sidney Crosby, Mario Lemieux, or even the current face of the league Connor McDavid. While only a few teams had a realistic shot at getting the number one pick, ultimately the Chicago Blackhawks came out on top and won the NHL Draft lottery and the opportunity to draft a franchise altering asset.
